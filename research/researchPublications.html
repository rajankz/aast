A Portrait of Japanese Americans

Produced by AAST and JACL

Through another non-profit, community-centered academic partnership, the Asian American Studies Program at the University of Maryland (AAST) and the Japanese American Citizen's League (JACL) proudly present A Demographic Overview of Japanese Americans.
This report provides a 60-page, full-color graphical analysis about the shifting demographic conditions of Japanese Americans across the U.S. The top ten Japanese American metropolitan areas are highlighted with perspectives from community members and statistical figures specific to each area based on the latest Census data (ACS 2009).

A Portrait of Chinese Americans

Produced by AAST and OCA

Through a unique national non-profit academic partnership, OCA and the Asian American Studies Program at the University of Maryland proudly present A Portrait of Chinese Americans.
The portrait provides a 64-page, full color portrayal and analyzes the latest statistics, charts, tables, and maps about the diversity of Chinese Americans. The top eight Chinese American metropolitan areas have profiles that include voices from these communities, maps showing Chinese American concentrations from 1970 to 2000, and future projections of Asian Americans in year 2012.
Following the national analysis is a presentation of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) concentration maps for the top eight communities of Chinese Americans in video format. Each of these areas are highlighted by the inclusion of spatial GIS concentration maps of the percentage and count of Chinese Americans from 1970 through 2000, as well as a map projection of the distribution of Asian Americans in 2012.
